The Five Alive Format

Five Alive is a competition management method for more than 8 athletes. A rolling group of 5 active athletes jump at all times. When someone clears or is eliminated, the next athlete from the wait list rotates in.

Two Events

This app manages Girls High Jump and Boys High Jump as separate events. Use the tabs at the top to switch between them. Each has its own athletes, heights, rotation, and results.

The Calling Order

  • UP — on the runway, about to jump
  • DECK — next up, approaching
  • HOLE / HOLD / 5TH — on standby with poles ready

Getting Started

You have two options:

  • Import PDF: Upload the meet program to auto-populate athlete rosters
  • Create Events Manually: Select which events (Girls, Boys, or Both) and add athletes one by one

Bar Heights Setup

Whether you imported or created events manually, set up the bar heights for your meet:

  • Starting Height — first bar height to jump
  • Increment — how much to raise after each height
  • Pre-load Count — how many heights to prepare in advance
💡 Heights are preserved per event (girls and boys can have different starting heights)

Adding / Managing Athletes

Tap 👥 Manage Athletes to view the full athlete list. From there:

  • Tap + Add Athlete to add new athletes one at a time or in bulk
  • Tap 🗑 Reset Athletes to clear all athletes and start over (useful if the PDF imported the wrong names)

The Check-In Screen

Before starting competition, athletes check in with the official. Tap any athlete card to check them in and record their declared starting height.

  • Athletes who don't check in are marked Not Competing and excluded from the rotation
  • The starting height dropdown shows your pre-loaded heights
  • Leave starting height blank to enter at the first height
💡 Use the search box to quickly find an athlete by name or school when they walk up.

Adding Athletes During Check-In

If an athlete arrives who's not on the roster, tap + Add Athlete to open the bulk add window and add them on the spot.

Starting Competition

Tap ▶ Start Competition when check-in is complete. Only checked-in athletes enter the rotation.

Recording Jumps

  • ✓ Make — cleared. Athlete moves to next height, new athlete rotates in.
  • ✗ Miss — knocked bar. After 3 misses, eliminated.
  • Ⓟ Pass — skips this height. Any misses carry forward as amber ⚠ dots at next height.

Check Out

Tap ⇄ Check Out when an athlete leaves for pole vault or another event. The app skips them until checked back in via the ↩ In button in the Jump Order panel.

Raising the Bar

Select an increment chip and tap Raise Bar. When only checked-out athletes remain, a yellow ⚑ Height Complete banner appears — you decide when to raise.

💡 Checked-out athletes automatically re-enter the rotation at the next height.

Ending the Competition

When all athletes finish, a red 🏁 Competition Complete banner appears and the End button pulses. Tap to finalize.

Exports

  • PDF — printable formatted results sheet
  • CSV — Hy-Tek compatible import file

View the scoresheet at any time via 📋 Scoresheet in the header — a Back button returns you to the active competition.

Automatic Data Saving

Five Alive automatically saves all your meet data to the browser — including athlete rosters, jump records, bar heights, and competition state. This protects your data in case of:

  • Loss of cell service
  • Accidental phone shutdown or app crash
  • Browser refresh or accidental navigation
💾 Your data is saved locally on this device and never uploaded anywhere.

Data Retention

Saved meet data persists for 30 days. This gives you time to finish the competition and make any corrections. After 30 days, the data is automatically cleared.

Running Multiple Meets

If you're an official running meets every week, you'll need to clear the old meet's data before starting a new one. Otherwise, the app will load your previous meet's data (athletes, heights, jump records, etc.) when you open it.

Clearing Data

When you're done with a meet and ready to start fresh for the next one, use the 🗑 Clear All Data button at the bottom of this help screen.

This completely resets the app and erases:

  • All imported athletes from both events
  • All jump records and competition data
  • Meet name and date
  • Bar height settings and saved state

After clearing, you'll be ready to import the next meet's PDF from scratch.

⚠️ This operation is permanent. There's no undo!
